This exercise uses Node.js to generate and receive data in a MySQL database.



This is customer.js

After initiating the node app my typing "node Bamazon.js" into the commande line we are immediatly given a list of all items for purchase.

Using the Id's given in the list, first select and item by its ID for purchase. Then select the quantity. Finally you specify your spending limit. Submitting this returns the before and after inventory of your item.

After purchase, you're left given an updated full list of items, the item you bought and how much it cost you. You'e also given the remaining amount of money you have as well as a list of all items in your cart.

As you buy more items your cart continue to grow until you run out of money or you choose to quit.

If you try to buy too many, an error is thrown explaining why you cannot.

This is manager.js

After initalizing BamazonManager.js in node You are given a set of options. Clicking option on displays a general inventory of all stock.

Option 2 gives you a minified inventory of all items with less than 5 items. Option 3 will allow you to add items to the inventory.

As you see, when we add items to the inventory with option 3, we no longer see them displayed with option 2.

Option 4 lets you add a new item into inventory. After filling out the appropriate information, you can see it displayed within inventory and will be treated as a new item.

As you can see the new item is in the inventory.
